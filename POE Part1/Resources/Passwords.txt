 Purpose of Passwords in Cybersecurity  
Passwords serve as the first line of defence in protecting digital assets. Their key purposes include:  

1. Authentication  
   - Verifies that a user is who they claim to be before granting access.  
   - Example: Logging into your email or bank account.  

2. Access Control  
   - Restricts unauthorized users from accessing sensitive data or systems.  
   - Example: Employees need passwords to access company databases.  

3. Data Protection  
   - Safeguards personal, financial, and confidential information from breaches.  
   - Example: A strong password prevents hackers from stealing credit card details.  

4. Account Security  
   - Prevents identity theft, fraud, and unauthorized transactions.  
   - Example: Without a password, anyone could log into your social media and impersonate you.  

5. Compliance with Security Policies  
   - Many organizations enforce password policies to meet cybersecurity regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA).
